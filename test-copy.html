The Problem

When requesting time off from work, people need to gather information from multiple places, which creates a disjointed experience.

Hypothesis

If Workday surfaced pertinent information such as time off balances, policy information, and team statuses, then it can provide a better customer experience.

Understanding the Problem

When I joined the team as an intern in 2017, the design team had been tackling problems with product parity across platforms and introducing a new design system. Loads of work had been done to ensure that design improvements were customer-centric.

By leveraging research studies from these efforts, we were able understand customer problems with the "request time off" feature of Workday.

I supplemented these studies by conducting product audits, competitive analyses, and qualitative user interviews.

Key Insights from Research

New hires were confused about the purpose and how PTO plans differentiated.
‍
People that work in collaborative environments value knowing when their teammates will be out of the office.

People take work deadlines or company events into consideration when planning time off.

ideation

Running a design jam with diverse stakeholders helped me rapidly form ideas and understand different perspectives of the product.

Ideas ranged from different ways of visualizing time off balances to leveraging data to surface trends.

COnverging on features

Based on our findings, we narrowed it down to three specific jobs to be done and we began to design them in parallel.

These “how might we questions” drove our design thinking.
