The Problem

People need to gather a lot of information before they can confidently request time off from work. However, the time off experience was disjointed and provided minimal utility.

Hypothesis

If Workday surfaced pertinent information at the right time, then it can provide a better customer experience.

Research Methods

Leveraging past research to delve deeper

When I joined in 2017, the team was in the midst of introducing a new design system and improving parity across the mobile and web products. Heaps of research had been conducted to ensure that these design changes were customer-centric.

By leveraging research studies from these efforts, we identified problems with the "request time off" feature of Workday.

I supplemented these studies by conducting product audits, competitive analyses, and user interviews.


Key insights included –

1. New hires were confused about how time off plans differed

2. People that work in collaborative environments value knowing when their teammates will be out of the office.

3. People often manage multiple calendars and take them into consideration when requesting time off from work.

Collaborative ideation

I led a design jam with diverse stakeholders to ideate ways of improving the platform based on the research findings we had compiled.

These “how might we questions” drove our design thinking.

1. How might we inform people of important changes to their time off plans?

2. How might we better communicate time off balances?

3. How might we inform people when their teammates are out of the office?
