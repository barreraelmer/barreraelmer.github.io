Product Design Intern, Summer 2017
Intro

Modernizing enterprise software to deliver better experiences for people at work.

As an intern on the Payroll, Time Tracking and Absence team, I designed for recent grads, who are new to the workforce. With the support of my team, I was fortunate enough to take lead on the project from research to tested prototype.

‍

Due to non-disclosure agreements, I can only provide a brief summary of my work. Contact me for an in-depth look at the design process.

01

Customer Problem

Recent grads gather heaps of information from multiple places before they can confidently request time off from work. How might we help recent grads see information between shared tasks?

Time off on desktop was functional, but lacked utility
02

Key Insights from Research

I conducted product audits, competitive analyses, stakeholder and customer interviews to further understand the nature of taking time off from work. Key insights from this research showed –

  • Recent grads were often confused about how different PTO plans differed
  • People that work in collaborative environments value knowing when their teammates will be out of the office.
  • When requesting time off, people often plan their absence around work deadlines and company events.
After conversations with stakeholders and new hires, we mapped out the typical workflow
03

Solution

Following multiple iterations and a round of user testing, I designed this final prototype. These "how might we" questions drove our design thinking:

  • How might we inform people of important changes to their time off plan?
  • How might we better communicate time off balances?
  • How might we inform people when their teammates will be out of office?
The typical workflow
04

Impact

In the end, the balance breakdown component was validated and put on the roadmap. The mobile calendar interface was handed off to the finance team because they were working on a similar use case.

‍

Outside of my immediate project, I also participated in an internal hackathon! Over the course of a day, my team researched, identified a product opportunity, and prototyped – Mentorship on Workday. We took 3rd place and pitched the idea to executives.

05

Lessons Learned

This was my first time working in a product team. Since this was a blue sky project, I was able to explore the ideal user experience, without having to consider technical and business constraints. While, this helped me go through an end-to-end design process.
