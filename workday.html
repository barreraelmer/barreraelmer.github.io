Workday
Product Design Intern, Summer 2017
Intro

Modernizing enterprise software to deliver better experiences for people at work.

As an intern on the Payroll, Time Tracking and Absence team, I was tasked with designing for the mobile time off experience. With the support of my team, I was fortunate enough to lead the project from research to tested prototype.

‍

The end result was a redesigned time-off dashboard on mobile. Due to NDA, I am unable to go in-depth with my process. Contact me if you'd like to learn more!

01

Customer Problem

Qualitative research studies showed that people would gather information from multiple places before they could confidently request time off from work.

‍

However, the "time off" product area of Workday provided minimal utility.

Time off on desktop was functional, but lacked utility
02

Key Insights from Research

I conducted product audits, competitive analyses, stakeholder and customer interview to further understand the nature of taking time off from work. Key insights from this research showed –

  • New hires were confused about how different PTO plans differed
  • People that work in collaborative environments value knowing when their teammates will be out of the office.
  • People take work deadlines and company events into consideration when planning time off.
The typical workflow
03

Solution

After multiple iterations and a round of user testing, I designed this final prototype. These "how might we" questions drove our design thinking:

  • How might we inform people of important changes to their time off plan?
  • How might we better communicate time off balances?
  • How might we inform people when their teammates will be out of office?

04

Impact

In the end, the balance breakdown component was validated and put on the roadmap. The mobile calendar interface was handed off to the finance team because they were working on a similar use case.

‍

Outside of my immediate project, I also participated in an internal hackathon! Over the course of a day, my team researched, identified a product opportunity, and prototyped – Mentorship on Workday. We took 3rd place and pitched the idea to executives.

05

Lessons Learned

Ayayayyaa. This was my first time working in a product team. Since this was a blue sky project, I was able to explore the ideal user experience, without having to consider technical and business constraints. While, this helped me go through an end-to-end design process.
