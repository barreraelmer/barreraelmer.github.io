Our UCSD

ROLE: COPYWRITER

Conform. Cram. Graduate. Flounder.

This is the mold that perpetuates most undergraduate experiences. ourUCSD takes a critical approach at redesigning the UC system.


My Role

This project was my introduction to many design methodologies. I learned issue mapping, parallel prototyping, proper critiquing methods, but most importantly, I learned to iterate. To iterate to find the proper problem, and then to find the proper solution. My main role as copywriter of the team was to document our process.

IDEATION



Our quarter-long critical design project prompted us to tackle an issue relating to UCSD. After making an issue map and the first brainstorming session, our group decided to focus on the university’s educational structure. From here we designed basic prototypes for two iterations to be evaluated at the first crit day. Ultimately, we wanted to challenge students to question the value of their education.


PROTOTYPING



Our first three prototypes: gamifying the typical university path, staging as recruiters, encouraging people to map their timelines at UCSD


ourUCSD


We realized our initial solution did not properly convey our intended message. They were too imposing. Our original intention was to challenge students to question the educational system. Is UCSD providing them a deserved education?

We started by brainstorming what our ideal education would look. In our ideal school, there would be no pressures in picking your major. Ample time would be given for students to explore. Students would engage in project-based, collaborative courses. They would be encouraged to pursue “passion projects” without the fear of falling behind in academics. These were the fundamentals we envisioned.
